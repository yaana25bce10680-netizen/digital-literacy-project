# Case Study: "Refund Request" UPI Scam 
What it is: It is a social engineering scam that makes use of 
UPI for duping people and getting them to make a transaction 
based on a false promise of a refund or winning a contest. 

How it happens: - Hook: The scammer poses as a seller on OLX or a customer 
care representative of a delivery app.
- Pressure: The scammer demands a prompt refund or 
immediate transaction via UPI.
- Trick: The scammer sends a "Request Money" instead of 
money via a UPI-based application such as GPay or PhonePe.
 - Manipulation: The scammer convinces the target to "Enter 
your UPI PIN to receive cashback."
- Theft: Once the PIN is entered by the target, money is 
deducted instead of being credited. 

Who are the people targeted by these scams? 
Young adults and students are generally targeted since they are more active 
users of online transactions and may be in a hurry or otherwise 
distracted. 

What are the consequences of these scams? 
Apart from the monetary loss suffered by the target of the scam, they also face 
emotional distress and face difficulties in getting the money 
back from the bank or registering a complaint with the police, 
which may take a period of months.
