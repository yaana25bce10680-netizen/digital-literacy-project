# Stay Safe Online: A VITian Guide

Stay Safe Online: A VITian Guide

1)	PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving: You do not need to enter the UPI PIN for receiving funds. In case you're prompted for the PIN, it is a phishing attempt.

2)	Verify the 'Verify' Icon: Before paying unknown merchants, check for the blue tick or 'verified merchant' logo.

3)	 App Locks Are a Must: Ensure you have a separate fingerprint or pattern lock for payment apps such as UPI or banking apps and not the same fingerprint or pattern as the phone lock.

4)	No Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Ensure you do not check balances or perform transactions on free public Wi-Fi. Instead, use mobile data.

5)	UPI Security: Always ensure that the Receiver's ID displayed is correct before you proceed with the money transfer.

6)	The 'Think Before You Click' Rule: In case you receive an SMS claiming that your electricity or bank account is blocked, do not click on the link. Instead, call the customer care number.

7)	The 24-Hour Rule: Before clicking on any deals or schemes offering high cashback, wait for 24 hours.

8)	The 'Report Immediately' Rule: In case you have been scammed, every minute is crucial.

•	Reporting Channel: National Cyber Crime Portal - cybercrime.gov.in

•	Helpline: Dial 1930 to freeze the transaction.
